Built from two strong, well-sourced reports. The agent-ready thread's technical claims are grounded in primary standards and major papers (arXiv, RFCs, spec sites); the federated thread in framework docs, FL literature and UK guidance.

# Open items carried from the sources
- **Context Hub** *(now resolved)*: the primary source is Andrew Ng's open registry [Context Hub](../frameworks/context-hub.md) (`andrewyng/context-hub`, MIT). It confirms the architectural point — a content/skills registry (curated, versioned docs and [agent skills](../glossary/agent-skill.md), with annotations-as-untrusted and author feedback) is distinct from [MCP](../standards/mcp.md) transport and [A2A](../standards/a2a.md) interoperability.
- Federated LLM fine-tuning evidence in regulated UK settings is thinner than classical FL; named UK local-authority FL deployments are sparse.
